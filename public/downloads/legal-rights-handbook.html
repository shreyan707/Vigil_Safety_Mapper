Legal Rights Handbook

This quick handbook is designed to help a survivor preserve evidence, contact the right authorities, and ask for support without needing to remember every step during a crisis.

If there is immediate danger, call emergency services first. Collecting records matters, but immediate safety comes before paperwork.

1. What to save

  • Screenshots of messages, threats, calls, and social media activity.
  • Dates, times, locations, and names of witnesses.
  • Photos of injuries, damaged property, or relevant surroundings.
  • Copies of complaint acknowledgements, FIR details, or medical records.

2. First reporting options

  • Police emergency: 100
  • Women helpline: 181
  • Domestic violence support: 1091
  • Cybercrime reporting: cybercrime.gov.in or 1930

3. Questions to answer when filing a complaint

  • Who caused harm or made the threat?
  • What happened, in your own words?
  • When and where did it happen?
  • Is the person still nearby or still contacting you?
  • Do you need urgent medical care, transport, or shelter?

4. Safe evidence handling

  • Do not edit screenshots before saving originals.
  • Email copies to yourself or a trusted person.
  • Write down passwords or backup codes in a safe place.
  • Keep one offline copy of essential phone numbers.
