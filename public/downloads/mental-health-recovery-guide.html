VIGIL Guide

Mental Health Recovery Guide

Recovery starts with regulation, rest, and connection. Use this guide to stabilise the first few hours and days after a distressing event.

Grounding steps

  • Name five things you can see and four things you can touch.
  • Slow your breathing by making the exhale longer than the inhale.
  • Drink water and sit with both feet on the floor.
  • Move to a quieter space and reduce notifications if possible.

Small daily supports

  • Eat something simple at regular intervals.
  • Keep sleep and wake times as steady as you can.
  • Write down racing thoughts instead of trying to hold them all.
  • Ask one trusted person to check in with you.

Professional support prompts

  • Tell the provider what happened and when symptoms began.
  • Describe panic, dissociation, sleep disruption, or appetite changes.
  • Mention if the source of harm is ongoing so care can be planned safely.
  • Bring a trusted person if remembering details feels hard.

Seek urgent care now if

  • You feel unsafe with yourself.
  • You cannot stay oriented to time, place, or people.
  • You cannot eat, rest, or manage basic care.
  • You are having repeated panic episodes without support nearby.
